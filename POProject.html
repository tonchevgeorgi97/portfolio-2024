Introduction

Within the dynamic landscape of procure-to-pay solutions, purchase orders stand as a crucial element. At Payhawk, we've long empowered our customers with comprehensive tools to process and pay invoices, as well as the ability to manage suppliers. However, recognising the pivotal role of purchase orders, we embarked on a journey to enhance our platform. By incorporating a purchase order system, we were aiming at extending the capabilities of our platform to provide control over the entire procure-to-pay process like no other competitor at that time did.

The Problem Space

Our product lacks a Purchase order system, which creates compliance issues for our clients and reduces the level of automation we provide. This ultimately decreases the value of our service to our users.

The Solution

We addressed this problem by designing and implementing a comprehensive PO system. Our solution enables seamless PO creation through customizable workflows and PO document generation using a template. Crucially, It secures accuracy by executing the essential three-way matching process, aligning the invoice amount with the PO amount and verifying the ordered quantity against the goods received.

The Process

To tackle the design challenge, I started my discovery journey by conducting an extensive desk research. This initial phase was crucial in cultivating a deep understanding of the procure-to-pay process.

Following the desk research, I undertook a comprehensive competitive analysis to gain insights into how other solutions were addressing similar needs. This analysis provided valuable benchmarks and highlighted potential opportunities for innovation within our own product.

Upon further exploration, integrating a purchase order system revealed itself as a complex task, affecting multiple product areas significantly. Understanding the scale of this challenge and its potential impact on our product's core functionality, I opted for a structured approach to navigate the challenge effectively. I proposed facilitating a Google Design Sprint—an intensive, collaborative workshop that would enable us to rapidly iterate through ideas, validate assumptions, and align stakeholders towards a shared vision.

The Google Design Sprint consisted of 5 phases that we distributed in 5 working days each of them with the following agenda:

Day 1 - Understand: Problem definition, Goal, Target
Day 2 - Sketch: Ideation, critical thinking solution space
Day 3 - Decide: Narrow down solutions and storyboard building
Day 4 - Prototype: High fidelity prototype of the solution
Day 5 - Test: Collect feedback & reactions

Design

After finishing the design workshop all stakeholders were aligned towards the vision for the needed solution and we managed to gather valuable insights on the user’s intentions and expectations for the desired product feature. We managed to test a high fidelity prototype with potential users and gather meaningful feedback on the way we can improve our experience. We found out what are the must have functionalities of a purchase order system suitable for our client’s use cases and helped us nail down our MVP that was going to enable us to compete with the best solutions in the procure to pay space.

The prototyped solution went through a few more iterations, was validated by dozens of clients and was examined and challenged multiple times within the product, marketing and engineering teams.

Today, the result stands as a fully functional solution, built upon the foundation of these collaborative efforts and user feedback, ensuring that it effectively meets the needs and expectations of its intended audience.
